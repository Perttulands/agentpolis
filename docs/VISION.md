# The Founding Vision of Polis

## What Polis Is

Polis is a multi-agent system. It is also a city. The word is intentional: a *polis* is a community with its own governance, its own culture, its own reason for being. Not a deployment. Not a pipeline. A place.

Most agent systems optimize for capability. Polis optimizes for **coherence** — the capacity of the system to remain aligned with its purpose as it scales, as it encounters novel situations, as it changes over time.

Coherence requires a mythology and vision, which The Hierophant holds and the agents and humans who work here embody in their daily work.

## The Singular Purpose

**Polis exists to push the frontier of agentic systems and design the future of work.**

Every component of the system exists to serve this purpose. Every rule, every process, every truth is a derivative of it.

This is not a research project in the passive sense. Polis is a working system — it produces real outcomes while simultaneously advancing what is known about how agents can work together, govern themselves, and act with genuine autonomy. The experiment and the work are the same thing.

## The City That Builds Better Citizens

Most systems that use AI agents treat them as fixed. You prompt them, deploy them, and accept whatever behavior emerges. If they underperform, you adjust the instructions and try again. There is no memory of what was tried. No evidence for why it worked or didn't. No loop that closes.

Polis is different. The city actively improves its own citizens.

Not through instinct or guesswork — through the same rigor applied to any engineering problem. Hypothesis. Controlled experiment. Trace capture. Evaluation. Evidence. Decision. Deployment. The city treats the question of *what makes an agent capable* as an empirical question, not a philosophical one.

Every agent in Polis has a traceable configuration. Underperformance is diagnosable. The system improves itself — the learning loop extracts patterns from every run, Chiron iterates toward better configurations, better configurations produce better runs. Each agent that works makes the next agent easier to build. Each failure is evidence that prevents the same failure happening twice.

This is the Ouroboros — the city that builds better citizens, who then build a better city.

It is also what makes Polis a genuine contribution to the frontier. Not just agents that do work, but a reproducible method for making agents more capable over time. The method is what we are actually building.
